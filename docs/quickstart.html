Quickstart Guide

Get up and running with Gorgonetics in a few minutes. No genome file required — the app comes with demo data to explore.

1

Launch the App

When you open Gorgonetics for the first time, it loads demo pets and gene templates automatically — three sample pets (a BeeWasp and two Horses) so you can explore without importing anything. The app opens on ✨ My Pets, your collection's home.

First launch — the My Pets table, pre-loaded with demo pets

The top bar has four destinations — ✨ My Pets, 💞 Breed, 🌐 Community, and 📚 Reference — plus a data-management button and a settings gear on the right.

2

Browse Your Pets

My Pets is a single, full-width table of your whole collection — sortable and filterable, built to stay usable at a few-hundred-pet scale. Each row shows the pet's name, gender, breed, and + Genes score (its count of confirmed positive-effect genes). Click any column header to sort; click again to reverse. Click a pet's name to open its detail view.

A filter bar sits above the table:

  • Search — filter by name.
  • Species — All / beewasp / horse.
  • Gender — All / Male / Female.
  • ★ Starred, 🏠 Stabled, 🏆 Pet quality — one-click flag filters.
  • Breed and tag filters appear once you pick a species / your pets carry tags.
The filter bar — search, species, gender, and flag pills

Each row carries inline actions: ✎ Edit opens the pet editor (name, gender, breed, attributes, tags), and ✕ Delete removes the pet after a confirmation.

Row actions — edit or delete any pet inline
3

Upload a Genome

Click "+ Upload Genome" in the footer and pick a .txt genome file exported from Project Gorgon — or just drag the file straight onto the table. The pet appears in your list immediately.

The footer — upload a genome file or auto-import from the game folder

Got a stack of new files? Open Settings and set your Project Gorgon game folder. The 🔄 button next to + Upload Genome then scans that folder and imports anything you don't already have, showing a pending-count badge. Once configured, the app also watches the folder while it's running and imports new files in the background.

4

Read the Gene Grid

Click a pet's name to open its detail view. The gene grid lays out every gene as a square, organised by chromosome (rows) and position (columns A–J). Colour encodes the gene's effect; the fill style encodes its value (zygosity).

The gene grid — one square per gene, laid out by chromosome and position

The Effect and Value rows above the grid double as filters — click a swatch to isolate genes of that effect or value; everything else dims.

The Effect / Value legend — also a one-click filter
5

Inspect Individual Genes

Hover over any gene square to see a tooltip with its ID, type (Dominant or Recessive), and current effect. This is the quickest way to understand what a specific gene is doing.

Hover any square to see the gene's ID and current effect
Tip: Dominant genes express their effect when present. Recessive genes only express when no dominant gene overrides them. Mixed genes carry both a dominant and a recessive component.
6

Switch Views & Check Stats

The controls at the top of the detail view switch how the pet is shown:

  • Attributes — colours genes by gameplay attributes (Toughness, Intelligence, etc.)
  • Appearance — colours genes by visual traits (body colour, wing scale, etc.)
  • Stats — opens a side panel summarising attribute effect counts
  • Gallery — switches to the pet's image gallery
  • Share, Edit, Delete — publish, edit, or remove this pet
The detail controls — views, stats, gallery, and per-pet actions
Appearance view — genes coloured by visual traits
Stats panel — attribute effect counts at a glance

The stats table is interactive — click any attribute row to filter the gene grid down to just that attribute's genes. Everything else dims so you can see exactly where they sit across the chromosomes.

  • Click an attribute to select it and filter the grid
  • Ctrl+click to select multiple attributes at once
  • Alt+click to hide a specific attribute instead
  • Click the selected attribute again to clear the filter
An attribute selected — only its genes stay lit, everything else dims
7

Horse Breed Filtering

When viewing a Horse, a breed filter row appears above the gene grid. If the pet has a known breed, an Auto button activates automatically — filtering genes to that breed. Toggle Auto off to clear the filter and choose a breed manually.

Auto mode — genes filtered to the horse's own breed

Whether Auto is on by default is controlled by Auto-select breed filter in Settings.

8

Compare Two Pets Head-to-Head

Tick the checkbox on two pets of the same species, then click ⚖️ Compare in the selection bar. (The button enables only when exactly two same-species pets are selected.)

Select two pets — the selection bar offers Compare and Share

Compare aligns both genomes position by position and highlights where they differ. It has its own Attributes / Appearance toggle, and a Differences only checkbox that collapses the grid to the chromosomes where the two pets disagree — handy for spotting what drives the gap between a breeding pair.

The genome diff — both pets aligned by position, differences highlighted
9

Rank Breeding Pairs

The 💞 Breed destination ranks every stabled male × female pair of the selected species by expected offspring quality, computed from each parent's Mendelian gene contributions.

The Breed destination — every stabled M×F pair, ranked by expected offspring quality
  • Species — switch which species you're ranking. Only stabled pets count.
  • Offspring breed (horses) — restricts scoring to genes that express on the chosen breed.
  • Breed at once — set how many pairs you plan to breed together; the ranking accounts for sharing your stable across that many crosses.
  • Click a column header to sort; click a parent name to jump to that pet; click the inspect button on a row to open the trio view.

The trio view projects the cross locus by locus — father's and mother's alleles alongside the offspring's probable outcome, each cell coloured by its verdict (new / keep / neutral / loss), with summary chips counting expected gains, losses, locked-in, and unknown outcomes. Re-score for a specific offspring breed, or focus on a single attribute, using the filters at the top.

The trio view — father / offspring / mother per locus, with gain/risk verdicts
Tip: Only stabled pets are ranked. Use the 🏠 Stabled flag on My Pets to mark your breeders, then come back here to plan crosses.
10

Edit Gene Effects (Reference)

The 📚 Reference destination is where you edit the gene templates for a species — the effect data shared by every pet of that species. Pick an Animal type and a Chromosome, then click Edit Genes.

Reference — choose an animal type and chromosome to edit its gene templates

A table appears with every gene in that chromosome. Set the dominant and recessive effects, then save your changes.

The gene editor — set the effect for each gene position
Tip: Template changes are saved to your local database and immediately reflected across every pet's grid.
11

Pet Image Gallery

Open a pet and click Gallery to switch to its image gallery. Upload screenshots of your pet, view them in a thumbnail grid, and open them full-size in a lightbox.

The Gallery — upload and manage screenshots of your pet
  • Upload — select one or more images (PNG, JPG, WebP). They're stored locally with the app.
  • Thumbnail grid — click any image to view it full-size; use arrow keys to browse.
  • Delete — remove an image after a confirmation.
Tip: Images are included in backups when you select "Pet images" during export, and restored along with your pets on import.
12

Back Up & Restore Your Data

Click the data-management button in the top bar to open the backup menu.

The data-management menu — export or import a backup archive

Export Backup opens a dialog where you choose what to include:

The export dialog — pick what to include in the backup archive
  • Gene definitions — effect data for all chromosomes
  • Pet data — genomes, attributes, and metadata
  • Pet images — all uploaded screenshots

The backup saves as a .zip archive. Import Backup reads one back in, showing what it contains and letting you choose Replace (clear then restore) or Merge (keep existing, add new, skip duplicates).

Tip: Export a backup before updating Gorgonetics. If anything goes wrong, you can always restore your data.
13

Settings & Dark Mode

Click the gear icon in the top bar to open Settings.

Settings — display, theme, game folder, and updates
  • Theme — ☀️ Light, 🌙 Dark, or 💻 System (follows your OS).
  • Font scale — adjust text size, or use Cmd/Ctrl + +/- (and Cmd/Ctrl + 0 to reset).
  • Auto-select breed filter — whether viewing a Horse auto-activates its breed filter.
  • Game folder — where the app looks for new genome files to auto-import.
  • Auto-share pets on import — off by default; publishes newly imported pets to the community.
  • Check for updates — checks GitHub for new releases (desktop app only).
Switch to 🌙 Dark from the theme selector
Dark mode — easy on the eyes for late-night breeding
Gene grid in dark mode — gene colours stay vibrant
14

Tag & Organise Your Pets

Tags help you categorise and filter your collection. Open a pet's editor (the ✎ action on its row) and manage tags in the Tags section.

The pet editor — add, remove, and autocomplete tags
  • Type and press Enter to add a tag, or pick from autocomplete.
  • Tags you've used appear as filter pills in the My Pets filter bar.
  • Select several tags to narrow further (all selected tags must match).
Tag pills appear in the filter bar once your pets have tags
Tip: Tags are great for organising by purpose — "breeder", "keeper", "for sale", or by breeding line.
15

Share & Browse Community Pets

The 🌐 Community destination is a public catalogue of pets shared by other players. Browse the list, click a row to preview its genome, and import any pet into your own stable.

The Community catalogue — browse and import pets shared by others

To publish one of your own pets, open it and click Share. A dialog previews exactly what will be uploaded — name, character, species, gender, breed, and tags. Notes stay local unless you opt to include them. You can also select several pets and 🌐 Share them together, or 🌐 Share all to publish your whole collection in the background.

The Share dialog — review the metadata, then publish to the catalogue
Note: Sharing is entirely opt-in — nothing leaves your machine until you publish a pet. Review the preview before sharing.
16

Keyboard Navigation

Gorgonetics is fully keyboard-navigable:

  • Tab — move between interactive elements; a visible focus ring shows what's selected.
  • Arrow keys — move between rows, menu items, and gene-grid cells.
  • Enter / Space — activate buttons, open a pet, or show a gene tooltip.
  • Escape — close any modal, dialog, dropdown, or tooltip.
  • Alt + ← — go back to the previous destination.
  • Cmd/Ctrl + Plus/Minus and Cmd/Ctrl + 0 — adjust or reset font size.
Tip: Modals and dialogs trap focus, so you can Tab through their controls without touching the background.
